**Date:** 2026-06-21  
**Target repo:** `/home/colebienek/projects/peptideOS`  
**Purpose:** fresh category-level landscape update to complement the existing one-to-one competitor analyses in `/docs`.  
**Evidence basis:** public App Store pages and search results, Google Play pages and search results, official product sites, and existing local PeptideOS competitive-analysis docs. This is a point-in-time snapshot. App metadata, ratings, pricing, copy, and shipped features change frequently.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The peptide-tracker category is no longer a sparse field of simple calculators plus a few serious tracking apps. As of 2026-06-21, the market is crowded and moving quickly toward the same product promise:

> a private protocol cockpit for peptides, GLP-1s, TRT/HRT, and adjacent compounds that connects schedules, dose logs, injection sites, inventory, symptoms, biomarkers, estimated levels, calculators, and AI-assisted interpretation.

PeptideOS should not assume its differentiation is simply "tracker plus calculator" or even "local-first tracker." Those claims are becoming table stakes across many competitors.

PeptideOS's defensible lane should be:

> **transparent, local-first protocol infrastructure with editable compound data, auditable assumptions, exportable records, AI-assisted operations, and a full research-to-execution loop.**

The most important update is to treat the market as a crowded, fast-copying application category, not a small set of isolated named rivals.

---

## 2. What Changed

The existing docs correctly identified the shift away from standalone reconstitution calculators toward protocol operating systems. The fresh update is that this shift has accelerated.

- Many apps now claim dose scheduling, protocol logs, reminders, and injection-site tracking.
- Estimated half-life, active-level, and medication-level charts are becoming common.
- Privacy, no-account, and local/offline-first positioning are common, especially among iOS-first apps.
- Compound libraries, research references, AI assistants, and insight dashboards are proliferating.
- GLP-1 and TRT positioning is being folded into peptide trackers because those markets have clearer mainstream demand.
- New apps are launching quickly, and App Store "You Might Also Like" surfaces many near-clones.

The strategic implication: PeptideOS needs stronger proof of trust, model quality, data portability, and workflow depth. A generic "all-in-one peptide tracker" will be hard to defend.

## 4. Emerging Table Stakes

These capabilities now appear often enough that PeptideOS should treat them as expected, not differentiating, unless implemented unusually well.

1. **Reconstitution and syringe-unit calculator**  
   Common across nearly every competitor. Differentiation requires saved calculations, clear arithmetic, broad unit/container support, and integration into inventory/logging.

2. **Schedules and reminders**  
   Expected in protocol trackers. PeptideOS needs missed/skipped/late state, schedule edits, phase/titration support, and a PWA-compatible reminder strategy.

3. **Dose log**  
   Expected. Differentiation requires fast logging, backfill, scheduled vs unscheduled entries, immutable history, and links to vials, sites, symptoms, and levels.

4. **Injection-site tracking**  
   Increasingly common. PeptideOS can still win if its taxonomy, rotation rules, reaction notes, and protocol-specific history are deeper.

5. **Inventory and vial runway**  
   Common in serious apps. PeptideOS should connect inventory to planned schedules and actual logs, not keep it as a passive list.

6. **Estimated level / half-life charts**  
   Quickly becoming a centerpiece. PeptideOS must ship this with visible assumptions, citation-backed PK metadata, planned-vs-actual views, and explicit "estimated remaining amount" language.

7. **Symptoms, metrics, labs, and Health data**  
   Common in advanced apps. PeptideOS should start with structured manual logs and overlays before promising medical-grade interpretation.

8. **Privacy / no-account / local-first claims**  
   Now common. PeptideOS must prove this through export, restore, sync status, conflict handling, and no silent data loss.

9. **Compound library**  
   Common. PeptideOS's opportunity is editable, versioned, citation-backed, model-ready data rather than just more profiles.

10. **AI assistant / insights**  
   Emerging quickly. PeptideOS should focus Peppi on operational actions and source-backed explanations, not generalized medical advice.

## 5. Differentiation Opportunities For PeptideOS

### 5.1 Transparent Model Quality

Competitors increasingly show "levels," "active amount," or "plasma curves." PeptideOS can differentiate by making model assumptions inspectable:

- half-life source and evidence tier,
- plasma half-life vs effect-duration distinction,
- route/site assumptions,
- planned vs actual dose event selection,
- unknown or conflicting evidence handling,
- no invented precision for weakly supported compounds.

This is stronger than claiming better curves. It shows why users should trust the curve.

### 5.2 Editable Protocol Infrastructure

The category is full of attractive native apps, but many will be constrained by fixed product models. PeptideOS can own:

- user-defined compounds,
- stack/protocol templates,
- phase-aware schedules,
- preparations/blends as first-class objects,
- linked inventory containers,
- deterministic JSON/CSV exports,
- importable public calculators.

The product should feel like protocol infrastructure, not just a diary.

### 5.3 Research-To-Execution Loop

PeptideOS has a natural path from reference data to action:

```text
compound library -> cited metadata -> calculator -> stack/protocol -> schedule -> dose log -> inventory depletion -> estimated levels -> symptoms/labs timeline -> export/report
```

Competitors usually own pieces of this loop. PeptideOS should make the connections explicit.

### 5.4 Peppi As Operations, Not Generic Advice

AI is becoming a crowded feature. PeptideOS should avoid "coach" and "safe stack" overclaiming and focus Peppi on:

- parsing a user-supplied protocol into editable schedules,
- adding inventory from plain language or photos later,
- explaining app data and calculations,
- summarizing what changed in a protocol,
- generating export/report drafts from user records,
- citing reference-library data when answering compound questions,
- requiring confirmation before mutating user data.

### 5.5 Data Ownership As A Product Feature

"Private" is common copy. PeptideOS can turn privacy into operational trust:

- local-first canonical storage,
- visible backup status,
- one-click full export,
- restore testing,
- sync conflict transparency,
- record-level timestamps/revisions,
- migration/version metadata.

This is more defensible than a generic privacy promise.

## 6. Positioning Update

Avoid positioning PeptideOS as:

- "the all-in-one peptide tracker,"
- "the safest stack planner,"
- "the AI peptide coach,"
- "the best peptide calculator."

Those are either crowded, medically risky, or easy to copy.

Recommended positioning:

> **PeptideOS is a local-first protocol cockpit for people who need their compound library, calculators, schedules, dose logs, inventory, symptoms, labs, and estimated levels to stay connected and auditable.**

Shorter variant:

> **Private protocol tracking with transparent calculations and portable data.**

Product-language guardrails:

- Prefer "estimated remaining amount" over "blood level" unless measured lab data exists.
- Prefer "cautions," "known interactions," or "evidence notes" over "safe stack."
- Prefer "reference schedule" over "recommended protocol."
- Prefer "source-backed educational summary" over "medical guidance."
- Do not imply dose, route, product, or schedule recommendations.

## 7. Updated Priority Stack

The existing docs produce several valid but competing priority lists. The reconciled category-level order should be:

### Priority 1: Trust Foundation

- Full JSON export/import.
- Backup/restore UX.
- Migration metadata.
- Clear local-first storage language.
- No silent data-loss path.

Rationale: many competitors claim privacy; trust must become concrete before PeptideOS asks users to log sensitive longitudinal data.

### Priority 2: Connected Protocol Timeline

- Scheduled, logged, skipped, missed, and unscheduled dose events.
- Inventory changes.
- Injection sites.
- Notes/signals.
- Timeline filters by stack/protocol/compound.

Rationale: every serious competitor is converging on "whole protocol in one place." PeptideOS needs a unified operational surface.

### Priority 3: Estimated Levels With Citations

- PK metadata schema in compound records.
- Pure calculation functions with tests.
- Planned-vs-actual level chart.
- Evidence and assumption display.
- Careful safety language.

Rationale: levels are becoming table stakes, but transparent levels can still differentiate.

### Priority 4: Protocol-Linked Inventory Runway

- Container/vial assignment to schedules/logs.
- Depletion forecasts.
- Low-stock warnings.
- Blends/preparations model.

Rationale: inventory is where protocol tracking becomes practical and sticky.

### Priority 5: Public Calculator Funnel

- Crawlable reconstitution calculator.
- Half-life / estimated remaining amount calculator.
- Vial runway calculator.
- GLP-1 label schedule calendar with careful label/reference framing.
- Save/import into PeptideOS.

Rationale: acquisition matters, but the product must have a stronger retained workflow behind the funnel.

### Priority 6: Peppi Operations Layer

- Parse protocol text into editable drafts.
- Add/update inventory drafts.
- Explain calculations and data provenance.
- Summarize logs/timeline.
- Source-backed compound Q&A.

Rationale: AI should reduce workflow friction and improve auditability, not create medical-advice risk.

## 10. Bottom Line

PeptideOS is entering a fast-copying, crowded category. The market has validated the product thesis, but it has also compressed the obvious feature gap.

The path forward is not to out-checklist every iOS tracker. It is to become the most trustworthy and inspectable protocol system:

> **local-first records, transparent calculations, editable source-backed compound data, connected protocol timelines, operational inventory, and AI that acts on user-confirmed data rather than making medical claims.**
